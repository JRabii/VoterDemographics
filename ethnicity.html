Exploring the Characteristics of American Voters in 2018

Of Votes and Voices

A Contemplation on Representation

With the world of politics so ceaselessly charged with racial tension, we thought it was crucial to take a look at the data surrounding minority registration and turnout.

What we discovered was more surprising than we expected. The largest minority population in the U.S.--that of Latino--is still dwarfed by the majority by a ratio of nearly 5:1. In fact, even when combined, the total number of Black, Asian, and Latino people living in America amounts to less than 50% of the majority population.

Other Interesting Finds from the Data
  • The Latino to Asian population ratio is 3:1, but they're matched in terms of turnout.
  • 94% of Blacks in America are eligible to vote while only 70% of Asians or Latinos can.
  • 53% of Asians 54% of Latinos who were eligible actually registered to vote in 2018.
  • Meanwhile, 20% more blacks than Asians or Latinos registered to vote in 2018.
  • The population to representation ratio is lowest for Asians and Latinos.
  • 64%, 60% and 38% of Whites, Blacks, and Asians/Latinos actually have a voice in America's democracy.
